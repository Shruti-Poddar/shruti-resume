What I contributed
For Franklin Templeton, I developed multiple copy lines for social campaigns requiring brevity, clarity and a tone that felt intelligent without being inaccessible. Lines were reviewed and shortlisted by the senior copywriting team.

For Mother's Recipe hoarding copy: an entirely different discipline. Outdoor gives you two seconds and zero context. Every word has to earn its place.
"Share your playlist, not your password."
"If it looks like bait, better wait."

Social and outdoor copy require fundamentally different thinking, even when the brand and insight are the same. Both begin from a sharp consumer truth.

What I did
For a brief involving Himalaya, I conducted primary consumer interviews to understand how people actually perceived the brand, not what the brand assumed. This meant going out, asking questions, and listening carefully enough to distinguish what people said from what they actually meant.

What the shelf communicates: retail audit and consumer research for Naturell

The project
A structured research engagement for Naturell, a health and wellness brand. Goal: understand in-store performance: how visible the brand was at point of sale, how well-placed products were relative to competitors, and how consumers responded at the shelf.
What I did
Conducted in-store audits across 14+ retail locations, studying SKU placement, shelf positioning and signage. Then gathered consumer feedback to surface the gap between what the brand intended and what shoppers actually perceived at the moment of purchase.
"A brand can have the right product in the wrong place. Where something sits on a shelf is as much a brand decision as the packaging or the campaign."
Takeaway
Brand strategy does not end with the ad. Retail is a media channel. This project gave me a grounded understanding of how brand perception is shaped by physical presence, not just communication.
